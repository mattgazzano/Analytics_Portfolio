My Approach to Data Analytics

Understand the Problem Statement

Every successful analytics project starts with understanding the business needs and how the problem statement can be measured. This stage typically involves interviewing business stakeholders and understanding how an underlying OLTP source system operates within the organization. Throughout my career, I've worked closely in operational, digital campaign, and finance domains.

Extract, Load & Transform




Once it's clear what data is relevant and available to the questions we need to answer about the business, a proper ingestion and transformation process needs to take place inside the Data Warehouse. Ultimately, we need to make raw data from source systems meaningful to data analysts and other business users to build reporting on top of. I've had hands-on experience with ingestion tools like Apache Airflow, Dagster, and Data Transformation tools such as DBT. I've also been able to work extensively with Google Cloud Platform as an organization administrator.

Tell a Story




Most importantly, we need to provide valuable analysis and recommendation through a story. No matter how we deliver this (a Dashboard, Jupyter Notebook, Excel File, or even a PowerPoint), our reports need to be clear in the story that they are telling. It is also extremely important to understand what level of stakeholders we are solving for in our analysis (C-suite? Middle Manager? Business Users?) and tailor it to the questions that they will need to answer.
